# Training: Deck Themes

A **theme** is the deck-wide visual skin: page background, headline font,
dark-panel color, and whether the drafting grid shows. One click restyles
every slide at once — content never changes.

**In the app:** open any deck → toolbar → the **Theme:** dropdown (next to
Generate Deck). The choice saves immediately and applies to the editor, the
client presentation link, and the PDF.

---

## The five themes

| Theme | Page | Headlines | Dark panels | Grid | Feels like |
|-------|------|-----------|-------------|------|-----------|
| **Blueprint** (default) | Pure white | Jost (clean sans) | Slate | ✔ graph paper + corner brackets | The friendly architect's working set |
| **Editorial** | Warm linen | Cormorant (classic serif) | Deep navy | — | The NotebookLM magazine look |
| **Gallery** | Bright white | Playfair Display (sharp serif) | Charcoal black | — | Minimal modern luxury |
| **Coastal** | Sea-mist | Lora (warm serif) | Tidewater teal-navy | — | Lowcountry beach house |
| **Palmetto** | Cream | DM Serif (engraved display) | Deep palmetto green | — | South Carolina estate |

> **Tip:** match the theme to the client and the home. Coastal for
> oceanfront renovations, Gallery for modern builds, Palmetto when you want
> unmistakably-local charm, Editorial for the classic proposal feel.

---

## What a theme does NOT change

- **Your content** — text, photos, prices, and per-slide font/color
  overrides you've set in the inspector always win over the theme.
- **Brand orange** — the accent color stays HHI orange in every theme.
- **Design-identity art** — the dark Why Us guarantee grid, comparison
  columns, COPE stone columns/honeycomb, isometric icons, and ghost
  numerals keep their own look by design.
- **Dollar figures** — never touched by anything visual.

> **Heads-up:** if a slide doesn't seem to follow the theme, check whether
> a font or color was manually overridden in its inspector — clear the
> override (Reset) and the theme takes back over.

---

## Quick-start

1. Build the deck content first — theme last is fine; it's non-destructive.
2. Flip through the **Theme** dropdown with a content-heavy slide on screen
   (Investment by Space is a good test page).
3. Pick one and present. You can change it again at any time, even after
   publishing a new snapshot.
