# Training: The Design Experience Slide

The **Design Experience** slide is the project *journey* — the ordered stages a
client moves through, from the first design steps to a signed, fixed‑price
contract. It reassures clients that there's a clear, repeatable process behind
the project. There's one Design Experience slide per deck, and it's included by
default.

---

## 1. Where it lives

**In the app:** open a project → **Deck** tab → the **Design Experience** slide
(it seeds in automatically, positioned just before the Timeline).

---

## 2. Choose a layout

**In the app:** Inspector → **Layout**. Five options:

| Layout | Looks like | Best for |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Stepped Circles** | A row of big ringed icon‑circles joined by a bold connector bar; label above, description below | The signature look — great default |
| **Chevron Flow** | A row of orange chevron arrows, each with a stage label inside | A punchy, compact "pipeline" feel |
| **Serpentine Cards** | White cards connected by a snaking arc that loops over/under | An elegant, editorial journey |
| **Steps + Photo** | A project photo on the left, numbered step cards on the right | When you have a strong site photo |
| **Ladder + Photo** | Dark layout: big numbers + steps on the left, framed photo on the right | A bold, technical "blueprint" feel |

---

## 3. Edit the stages

**In the app (Inspector → Stages):**

- **Title** and **Description** for each stage — edit, reorder (▲▼), add, or remove.
- **Step Word** — the prefix before the number: **Stage / Stop / Phase / Step / None.**
- **Lock styles together** — keeps every stage's text styling consistent.

**Heads‑up — fields follow the layout.** The inspector only shows controls a
layout actually uses:

| Field | Stepped Circles | Chevron | Serpentine | Steps + Photo | Ladder + Photo |
|-------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Description | ✅ | — | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Icon | ✅ | — | — | — | — |
| Photo | — | — | — | ✅ | ✅ |

---

## 4. Icons (Stepped Circles)

Each stage shows an **isometric, two‑tone icon** by default (navy linework +
orange accents) — measure, feasibility, plans, selections, contract. To change one:

**In the app:** open a stage → **Icon** and pick a different built‑in icon, or:
- **Use default icons** (button at the top of Stages) — restores the built‑in
  isometric set on every stage in one click, without touching your text.
- **Custom / AI Icon** — pick an uploaded brand icon, or run ✦ **AI Edit →
  "generate an icon for each stage"** to create bespoke isometric icons.

> **Tip:** the built‑in defaults are crisp and instant — you usually don't need
> AI generation unless you want a very specific subject.

---

## 5. Size & spacing controls

**In the app (Inspector):**

- **Circle Size / Stage Size / Card Size** — one slider scales the main element
  for the active layout (the circles, the chevrons, or the cards) proportionally.
- **Step Label Size** — sizes just the "Stage 1:" … "Stage 5:" prefix,
  independently of the title.
- Per‑stage **Title** and **Description** size/color, plus accent color.
- **Project Photo** — for the Steps + Photo and Ladder layouts, choose the photo
  from the library.

---

## 6. Theme (matches the rest of the deck)

The Design Experience slide follows the deck **Theme** dropdown:

- **Blueprint** — white background, bold sans type, faint grid + corner brackets
  (the clean, modern look).
- **Editorial** — warm linen background, Cormorant serif (the soft, premium look).

Set the theme once for the whole deck; this slide adapts automatically.

---

## 7. AI Edit

**In the app:** the **✦ AI Edit** box at the top of the inspector. Plain English —
e.g. *"switch to the serpentine layout,"* *"tighten each stage to one line,"* or
*"generate an icon for each stage."* **↶ Undo** reverts the last change, and your
stage wording is preserved unless you explicitly ask to change it.

---

## 8. Quick‑start checklist

1. Open the **Design Experience** slide.
2. Pick a **Layout** (try **Stepped Circles**).
3. Tidy the **stages** (titles + descriptions) and set the **Step Word**.
4. On Stepped Circles, confirm the **icons** (or click **Use default icons**).
5. Nudge **Circle/Stage/Card Size** and **Step Label Size** to taste.
6. Confirm the deck **Theme** (Blueprint vs Editorial) reads the way you want.
