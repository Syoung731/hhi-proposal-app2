# Training: The Next Steps Slide

The **Next Steps** slide tells the client exactly what happens after the
presentation — sign the design contract, schedule the measure meeting,
complete the feasibility study, receive the proposed plan. It's the
"here's the path" slide that comes right before the Closing.

> **Heads-up:** contact info (email / phone / address) is **not** on this
> slide on purpose — it lives on the **Closing slide**, which immediately
> follows. Don't go looking for contact fields in this inspector; they
> aren't there.

---

## 1. Where the steps come from

New decks seed the steps from **Settings → Next Steps** (global defaults:
the step list, default layout, section label, and headline). Out of the box
that's the four HHI steps: **Sign the Design Contract → Schedule Your
Measure Meeting → Complete the Feasibility Study → Receive Your Proposed
Plan.** Edit them per-deck in the inspector; **Reset** restores the saved
defaults.

> **Tip:** if your sales team changes the standard process, update it once
> in **Settings → Next Steps** and every *new* deck picks it up.

---

## 2. Choose a layout

**In the app:** Inspector → **Layout**. Five options:

| Layout | Looks like | Best for |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Numbered + Photo** (default) | Project photo on the left, serif "01 / 02 / 03" numbered list on the right | The classic — pairs the path with a hero image |
| **Staircase Cards** | 3D slate cards climbing left→right with hard offset shadows, navy footer band with a tagline | A bold, architectural statement (no headline drawn — the tagline carries it) |
| **Column Grid** | Steps as equal columns with big numerals | 3–4 evenly weighted steps |
| **2×2 Grid** | Four white cards with number badges | Exactly four steps, clean and symmetric |
| **Large Number Hero** | Giant ghost numerals behind each step's text, footer slogan line | The premium "Path Forward" look from our reference decks |

---

## 3. Edit the steps

**In the app:** Inspector → **Steps**.

- **+ Add**, ✕ remove, and ▲▼ reorder.
- Each step has a **Number**, **Title**, and **Description**, and each of
  those has its own **Font / Size / Style (B-I-U) / Color / Outline**
  controls.
- **Lock all items to match first** — style step 1 once and every other
  step copies it. Leave it on unless you need one step to stand out.

> **Tip:** keep descriptions to one or two short sentences. Every layout
> draws all the steps — long paragraphs shrink the whole composition.

---

## 4. Layout-specific controls

**In the app (Inspector — sections appear only on the layout that uses them):**

- **Numbered + Photo → Photo:** pick any project/library photo for the left
  panel. With no photo the list centers on the slide instead.
- **Large Number Hero → Right Photo:** optional photo column. **Number
  Size** (per step) also scales the ghost numeral behind the text.
- **Staircase Cards → Staircase Layout:** the overlap controls —
  - **Card Width** — cards always spread edge-to-edge, so narrower cards
    overlap less. With 4 steps, **≈0.80×** removes the overlap entirely.
  - **Climb** — how steeply the staircase rises (spreads cards vertically).
  - **Lower cards in front** — flips the stacking so a card's text is never
    covered by the next card, no matter the sizes.
- **Staircase Cards / Large Number Hero → Footer Band / Footer Line:** the
  **Tagline**. Staircase default: *"Let's build your vision."* Hero default:
  *"Turning Ideas into Ideal Spaces. Design - Build - Remodel."* Clear the
  field to use the default; type a single space to hide the line.

---

## 5. Header controls

**Section Label** (default "WHAT HAPPENS NEXT") and the **Headline**
(default "Your Path Forward") each have full font/size/style/color
controls at the top of the inspector. Staircase Cards skips both by
design — its navy tagline band is the message.

---

## 6. Quick-start checklist

1. Pick a **Layout** (Numbered + Photo with a great project photo is the
   safe default; Staircase or Large Number Hero for more drama).
2. Confirm the **steps** read right for this client — tweak titles or
   descriptions if the project skips a phase.
3. Layout extras: choose the **photo**, set the **tagline**, or tune the
   **staircase** width/climb so every card is readable.
4. Let the **Closing slide** handle contact info — this slide just sells
   the path.
