# Training: The Cover Slide

The **Cover** is the first thing the client sees — a full-page hero photo of their
home with the project's concept title, who it's prepared for, and the HHI logo.
It sets the tone for the whole proposal.

---

## 1. Where it lives

**In the app:** open a project → **Deck** tab → click the **Cover** slide
(slide 1) in the left rail → edit it in the right-hand **Inspector**.

---

## 2. The two titles (this trips people up)

The cover has **two** pieces of title text, and they are *not* the same:

- **Headline** — the **big serif title**. This should be an evocative **concept
  name** for the project, not just the address. Think *"Enhanced Livability for
  94 Coggins Point,"* *"A Complete Reimagining,"* *"From Vision to Reality."*
- **Project Name** — the **small uppercase label** above/below the title (often
  the address or "Remodeling Proposal").

**In the app (Inspector):** the **Headline** field is the big title; the
**Project Name** field is the small label.

> **Heads-up:** When you (or the AI) "give the cover a concept title," that text
> goes in the **Headline** field — the big serif line. The address belongs in
> **Project Name** (or it shows automatically in the Prepared-for line).

---

## 3. Build it with AI (recommended)

**In the app — Deck tab → "Generate Deck" → AI Fill → "Draft slide copy."**

This writes a project-specific **concept title** into the Headline plus a short
**tagline**, pulled from the actual scope. So a fresh cover reads
*"Enhanced Livability for…"* instead of a generic "Project Proposal."

### AI Edit (the smart box)

**In the app:** select the Cover → the **✦ AI Edit** box at the top of the
Inspector → type what you want → **Apply AI Edit**. It figures out your intent.
Examples:

| You want… | Type something like |
|-----------|---------------------|
| A concept title | "Give this an evocative concept title about a coastal sanctuary" |
| A tagline | "Add a confident one-line tagline" |
| A new background image | "Generate a soft coastal-twilight background" |
| Swap the hero photo | "Use the front-elevation after-photo" |
| Recolor the accent | "Make the accent a deep bronze" |

> **Undo:** the **↶ Undo** button next to "AI Edit" steps back the last AI change
> (click again for earlier ones). Undo history clears if you reload the page.

> **Tip:** Generating a background image takes ~30s; text changes are instant.

---

## 4. The hero photo

The big background photo is the project's **hero image**.

**In the app:** set it on the **Build Presentation** tab (Hero photo) or the
**Media** tab; the cover always shows the latest hero. You can also tell **AI
Edit** to "swap the hero photo" and pick from the project's library.

> **Heads-up:** If the cover shows a grey "Cover Photo" placeholder, no hero is
> set yet — add one on Build Presentation/Media.

---

## 5. Choosing a layout

**In the app:** Inspector → **Layout**.

| Layout | Looks like |
|--------|-----------|
| **Split Editorial** | Full-bleed photo, dark overlay, centered white title |
| **Right Panel Overlay** | Photo with a translucent white info panel on one side |
| **Split Dark Editorial** | Dark brand panel on the left, full photo on the right |
| **Bottom Card Overlay** | Full photo with a floating white card in a bottom corner |
| **CAD Overlay** | Photo that fades into an architectural blueprint drawing |

Layout-specific controls appear when relevant — e.g. **Panel Side** (Right Panel),
**Tagline** (shows on the CAD Overlay layout), and the CAD source photo/intensity
(CAD Overlay).

> **Tip:** Switching layouts never loses your text or photo — try a few.

---

## 6. The details line: Prepared for / address / date

Every layout shows a **"Prepared for [client] · [address] · [date]"** block.

**In the app (Inspector):**

- **Prepared For** — the client's name.
- **Address** — defaults to the company address; type the **project site**
  address here. Use **Reset to company address** to revert.
- **Date** — free text (e.g. *March 22, 2026*).

> **Tip:** Always set **Address** to the project site, not the HHI office, so the
> cover reads "Prepared for the Smiths · 94 Coggins Point · …".

---

## 7. Logo, background & theme

- **Logo** — Inspector → **Show Logo** + position controls (managed in
  Settings → Branding).
- **Background** — Inspector → **Background** to pick a brand background, or use
  **AI Edit** to generate one from a prompt.
- **Accent Color** — Inspector → **Accent Color** (the rule/labels); defaults to
  brand orange.
- The cover follows the deck **Theme** (Editorial / Blueprint) set in the toolbar.

---

## 8. Quick-start checklist

1. Set the **hero photo** (Build Presentation / Media).
2. **Deck → Generate Deck → Draft slide copy** (writes the concept title + tagline).
3. Open the **Cover** slide; tweak the **Headline** (concept title) and **Project
   Name** (label) if needed — or use **AI Edit**.
4. Set **Prepared For**, **Address** (project site), and **Date**.
5. Pick a **Layout** and confirm the logo + theme look right.
